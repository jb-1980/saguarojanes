About us

Our Mission

We seek to follow the admonition of Jesus to his disciples as recorded in Matthew 14:16, "They do not need to go away. You give them something to eat." We will do this by actively working with organizations in our community to gather food to distribute to those who are hungry.

Our Story

Saguaro Jane's begin in the 1940s when a small group of women decided to assist the families of those who had loved ones who went off to war. Since then, our organization has grown to a group of {{INSERT NUMBER}} men and women volunteers who work tirelessly to provide food to our community.

True to our desire to help, our organization is composed of volunteers. No one is paid, and all donations go to helping out the hungry and needy through our various programs. Thanks to the generous donations of others, we are able to make a difference in our community and give something to eat to the hungry.

What we do

The main purpose of our organization is to feed the hungry. To accomplish this purpose we have a variety of programs that we are able to run thanks to the work of our tireless volunteers and generous donors.

  • Brown Bag Day
  • Special Programs
  • Holiday Programs
  • Thrift Store

Each Thursday, in cooperation with St. Mary's Food Bank, Saguaro Janes volunteers will prepare food boxes to be distributed to needing families. Starting at 10 AM, our volunteers help direct participants to their food boxes and if they need assistance will help carry their vehicles.

Thrift Store

Saguaro Jane's also has a thrift store where many residents shop weekly. Clothing, shoes, kitchen items, knickknacks, toys, linens and other items are donated. This store is maintained by volunteers and all monetary donations goes to pay expenses to keep the Saguaro Jane's center open.
